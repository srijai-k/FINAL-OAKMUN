Oakridge MUN · Chapter XVI

A Letter from the
Secretary-General

It is with immense pride and excitement that I welcome you to Oakridge Model United Nations, Chapter XVI. This conference represents not just a gathering of young minds, but a celebration of diplomacy, dialogue, and the enduring belief that thoughtful conversation can change the world.

Over the three days of 24th, 25th, and 26th July, delegates from across the country will come together to debate some of the most pressing issues of our time. Each committee has been carefully curated to challenge you intellectually and push the boundaries of your perspective.

MUN has always been more than a competition. It is a space where you learn to listen as much as you speak, to find common ground where little seems to exist, and to lead with empathy. I encourage every delegate to embrace that spirit fully — walk in with your positions, but leave room to be moved.

We have worked tirelessly to ensure this edition of Oakridge MUN is the finest yet. I look forward to seeing the debates unfold, the resolutions take shape, and most importantly, the friendships form.

Welcome to Chapter XVI.

FAQ

What is Oakridge MUN?

Oakridge Model United Nations (MUN) is an annual student-led conference hosted by Oakridge International School, Bengaluru. Delegates simulate United Nations committees, debate real-world issues, and collaborate to draft resolutions — developing skills in diplomacy, public speaking, and critical thinking.

Who can participate in Oakridge MUN Chapter XVI?

Oakridge MUN is open to students from Grade 6 through Grade 12 from any school. Whether you are a first-time delegate or a seasoned MUNner, there is a committee suited to your experience level. Delegates must register through their school or as an independent participant.

How do I register for the conference?

Registration is done through the official Oakridge MUN portal. Click "Register Now" at the top of this page, fill in your details, select your preferred committee, and submit your country preference. Confirmation emails with further instructions will be sent within 3–5 working days.

What should I prepare before the conference?

Each delegate is expected to research their assigned country's position on the committee's agenda topics and prepare an opening position paper. Study Guides will be shared by the Secretariat ahead of the conference. Delegates should also familiarise themselves with Rules of Procedure.

What is the dress code?

Western business formals are required for all three days of the conference. This means formal trousers, blazers, ties, or formal dresses/suits for delegates. Smart-casual is acceptable for the Social Evening on Day 2. Please ensure your attire is neat and presentable at all times.

How are awards decided?

Awards are evaluated by the Executive Board of each committee based on delegate performance across all sessions. Criteria include quality of speeches, contribution to debate, diplomacy, resolution drafting, and knowledge of subject matter. Awards include Best Delegate, Outstanding Delegate, Verbal Mention, and Best Position Paper.

Is accommodation provided for outstation delegates?

Oakridge MUN does not arrange accommodation directly, but we maintain a list of recommended hotels near the venue for outstation delegates and their families. Please reach out to the Secretariat via the contact form and we will share details to assist with your stay in Bengaluru.

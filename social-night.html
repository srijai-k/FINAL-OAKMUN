Day 2  ·  All Delegates Welcome
Dress Code

Traditionals.

Embrace the spirit of the Golden Solstice. Delegates are encouraged to wear their finest traditional attire — this is your moment to celebrate culture and heritage in full colour.

For Him
Traditional Menswear
  • Sherwani — preferred for the full look
  • Kurta with churidar or dhoti
  • Bandhgala / Nehru jacket with trousers
  • Coordinated dupattas and stoles welcome
  • Mojris, kolhapuris, or traditional footwear
Western formals are reserved for committee sessions. Tonight, go traditional.
For Her
Traditional Womenswear
  • Saree with blouse — all regional drapes welcome
  • Lehenga choli — any style, any colour
  • Anarkali suit or sharara
  • Salwar kameez with dupatta
  • Traditional jewellery and accessories encouraged
All regional and cultural traditions are equally celebrated — wear what represents you.
Evening Guidelines

Do's & Don'ts.

A few things to keep the Golden Solstice magical for everyone.

✓
Do's
  • ✓ Arrive in traditional attire — embrace the Golden Solstice theme fully
  • ✓ Be at the venue on time — the evening begins punctually
  • ✓ Dance, celebrate, and enjoy the performances wholeheartedly
  • ✓ Treat fellow delegates, performers, and staff with respect
  • ✓ Take photos and create memories — this is the night everyone talks about
  • ✓ Keep your delegate badge accessible for entry
✕
Don'ts
  • ✕ Arrive in western formals — tonight is about traditionals, not committee attire
  • ✕ Wear casual or informal clothing (jeans, t-shirts, sneakers)
  • ✕ Disrupt performances or talk loudly during acts on stage
  • ✕ Bring outside food or beverages into the venue
  • ✕ Leave the designated venue area without informing organisers
  • ✕ Engage in behaviour that disrupts the experience for other delegates

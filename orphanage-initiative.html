The Initiative

A seat at the table.
For everyone.

The Orphanage Initiative is one of the most distinctive parts of Oakridge MUN. Every year, children from a local orphanage are brought in as full participants — not as guests or observers, but as real delegates with a real committee.

This year, that committee is the United Nations General Assembly. They debate. They vote. They draft resolutions. The conference experience is identical to every other delegate's — because that's the whole point.

"They don't just watch the conference happen. They are the conference."

— Oakridge MUN Secretariat

What This Looks Like

Full delegates.
No exceptions.

01 — Their Own Committee
UNGA — Chapter XVI.

The children are assigned to the United Nations General Assembly — a fully structured committee with an agenda, chairs, and rules of procedure.

02 — Real Debate
They speak. They vote.

Sessions run identically to every other committee at the conference. Position papers, speeches, moderated caucuses — the full MUN experience.

03 — Mentorship
Guided every step.

Oakridge MUN delegates and faculty help prepare and support them throughout the conference — ensuring they feel confident and included.

04 — Recognition
Awards. Just like everyone else.

Best Delegate, Outstanding Delegate — the same awards that any other committee gives out. Their achievements are recognised on the same stage.

"

Diplomacy is only meaningful when it includes the people who need it most. That's what this initiative is about.
